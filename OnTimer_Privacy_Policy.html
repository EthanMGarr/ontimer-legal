OnTimer Privacy Policy

Last updated: [December 6, 2025]

Introduction

OnTimer (“we,” “our,” or “us”) provides a mobile application designed to help users stay on time for meetings by reading upcoming calendar events and delivering timely reminders. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information, including information accessed through your Google Account.

By using OnTimer, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the app.

If you have any questions, please contact us at: support@ontimer.app.

Information We Collect

1. Information You Provide

We may collect information that you provide directly, such as:

  • Your email address (for support or communication, if you provide it).
  • Reminder settings or preferences you configure within the app.

2. Information Accessed Through Google Calendar (Read-Only)

If you choose to connect your Google Account, OnTimer accesses the following read-only Google Calendar data:

  • Your list of Google calendars.
  • Event titles.
  • Event start and end times.
  • Event locations.
  • Event metadata (such as reminders or all-day flags).

OnTimer does not create new events, modify existing events, delete events, or share your Google Calendar data with third parties.

How We Use Google Calendar Data

We use Google Calendar data only for the following limited purposes:

  • To identify your calendars.
  • To read upcoming events.
  • To generate reminders before meetings.
  • To display event information inside the app.
  • To improve core app functionality and user experience.

We do not use your Google Calendar data for advertising, marketing, cross-user analytics, or sale to third parties.

All usage complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy , including the Limited Use requirements.

Local Device Storage

OnTimer does not store your Google Calendar event data on external or cloud servers. Event information is processed locally on your device and used solely to provide reminders and display your schedule.

We do not maintain separate server-side copies of your Google Calendar event data.

How to Disconnect or Revoke Access

You can disconnect your Google Account from OnTimer at any time by:

  • Using the account or calendar settings within the app, or
  • Visiting Google’s permissions page and revoking access: https://myaccount.google.com/permissions .

Once access is revoked, OnTimer will no longer be able to read or display your Google Calendar information.

Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or Google Calendar data. We also do not share your Google user data with third parties except in the following limited circumstances:

  • To comply with applicable law, regulation, legal process, or request.
  • To protect the rights, property, or safety of OnTimer, our users, or others.

We do not allow third-party advertisers or trackers to access your Google Calendar data through the app.

Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to help protect your information, including limiting data access to read-only Google Calendar scopes and processing calendar data locally on your device.

However, no method of transmission or electronic storage is completely secure. By minimizing server-side storage, we strive to reduce risk and exposure of your data.

Children’s Privacy

OnTimer is not intended for children under 13 years of age, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have received information from a child under 13, we will delete it.

Your Rights and Choices
